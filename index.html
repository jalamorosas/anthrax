What is Anthrax?

Anthrax is a serious bacterial disease of sheep and cattle, typically affecting the skin and lungs. It can be transmitted to humans, causing severe skin blemishes or forms of pneumonia. Anthrax is more likely to be found in animals in south and central America, south and east Europe, Asia and Africa.
